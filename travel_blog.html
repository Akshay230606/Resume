My Travel Blog

This travel blog describes the tourist places I have visited in chronological order and shares my personal experiences from each trip.

Mysore Trip (2 Days - 1 Night)

I visited Mysore with my father, mother, and brother for a 2-day and 1-night trip. Mysore is located about 100 kilometers from our home, and we traveled by our own car, which made the journey comfortable and enjoyable. The road journey itself was pleasant, and we enjoyed the scenery along the way.

On the first day, our main attraction was the Mysore Palace. The palace was very beautiful with its grand architecture, intricate designs, and rich history. I was amazed by the royal halls, paintings, and decorations.We have taken a printout of the family photo from one of the cameraman.

In the evening, we saw the palace illuminated with lights, which was a wonderful and memorable sight. Later, we visited the St. Philomena’s Church, which is one of the largest churches in India. The tall structure, stained glass windows, and peaceful atmosphere made the visit very special. It was a calm and spiritual experience for all of us.

On the second day, we went to the Mysore Zoo. It was one of the most exciting parts of the trip. We saw many animals such as lions, tigers, elephants, giraffes, and birds. The zoo was clean and well maintained, and I learned many interesting facts about wildlife.

We also visited Chamundi Hills, where we enjoyed a beautiful view of the city from the top. The cool breeze and natural surroundings made us feel relaxed. After lunch, we returned home with many happy memories. Overall, the Mysore trip was a memorable family experience. It helped me learn about history, culture, and nature, and spending time with my family made the journey even more special.

Ooty Trip (2 Days - 1 Night)

I visited Ooty with my family for a 2-day and 1-night trip. Ooty is a beautiful hill station located about 3 hours of travel from our home. We traveled by our own car, and the journey through the winding roads and greenery was very enjoyable. The cool climate made us feel relaxed as soon as we reached there.

On the first day, we visited the Ooty Botanical Garden, which had a wide variety of flowers and plants. The garden was well maintained, and walking through it was very refreshing. Later, we visited Ooty Lake, where we enjoyed boating and spent some peaceful time together. In the evening, we explored the local markets and tasted some homemade chocolates, which Ooty is famous for.

One of the most memorable experiences of the trip was seeing the Nilgiri Mountain Railway hill train, which runs from Mettupalayam to Ooty. Watching the train move through tunnels, bridges, and green hills was a wonderful experience, and it showed the beauty of the mountains in a unique way.

On the second day, we visited Doddabetta Peak, the highest point in Ooty. The view from the top was amazing, and we could see clouds moving around the hills. We took many photos and enjoyed the natural beauty before starting our return journey. Overall, the Ooty trip was a refreshing and memorable experience. The scenic beauty, cool climate, and family time made it a wonderful journey.

Ernakulam Trip (3 Days - 2 Night)

I visited Ernakulam with my family for a 3-day and 2-night trip. Ernakulam is about 7 hours of travel from our home, and we traveled by bus. The journey was comfortable and gave us a chance to relax and enjoy the view along the way.

On the first day, we visited Marine Drive, which is a popular waterfront area. Walking along the walkway and watching the backwaters was a peaceful experience. We also traveled using the Kochi Metro, which was clean, fast, and convenient. Later, we visited Lulu Mall, where we spent time shopping, exploring different stores, and enjoying food together.

On the second day, we experienced the Kochi Water Metro, which was a unique and enjoyable way to travel through the backwaters. The boat ride was smooth and offered beautiful views of the water and surroundings. After that, we visited Fort Kochi, where we saw the famous Chinese Fishing Nets. The place had a unique cultural atmosphere and historical importance. We also visited St. George Church, which was calm and beautiful.

On the third day, we visited Wonderla Amusement Park, which was one of the most exciting parts of the trip. We enjoyed many rides and attractions, including water rides and adventure rides. Spending the day at Wonderla was fun and refreshing, and it brought a lot of excitement to our trip.

The city had a mix of modern life and traditional culture, which made the trip very interesting. Traveling by bus, using the Metro and Water Metro, and spending time with my family made this trip very enjoyable. Overall, the Ernakulam trip was a pleasant and memorable experience that helped me understand the cultural diversity of Kerala.
